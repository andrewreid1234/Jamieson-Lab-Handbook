Troubleshooting

Adapted from Shimadzu's Liquid Chromatography Troubleshooting Guide. A few habits worth keeping before you start swapping parts:

  • Work out whether the problem is reproducible or intermittent — that changes where you look first.
  • Keep a record of what you tried; it's often the fastest way to spot a pattern later.
  • Check the obvious things first: leaks, air bubbles, correct column, correct method settings.
  • Change one variable at a time. If you swap a part to test it and that doesn't fix things, put the original back before trying the next thing.
