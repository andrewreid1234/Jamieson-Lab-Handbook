Troubleshooting

A few habits worth keeping before you start swapping parts:

  • Work out whether the problem is reproducible or intermittent — that changes where you look first.
  • Check the obvious things first: leaks, air bubbles, correct column, correct method settings.
  • Change one variable at a time, and put things back if a change doesn't fix it.

Most common issues:

SymptomLikely causeTry this
No peaksNo mobile phase flowCheck the purge valve isn't left open; purge the pump and injector.
Tailing peaksColumn degraded, or wrong mobile phase pHReplace the column; adjust pH away from the analyte's pKa.
Changing retention timesColumn not fully equilibratedUse at least 10 column volumes before starting.
Baseline drift or noiseAir or contamination in the detector flow cellFlush with isopropanol; check for leaks.
High pressureBlocked column, or wrong mobile phaseBackflush or replace the column; confirm mobile phase compatibility.
Fluctuating pressureAir bubbles, or worn pump sealsPurge solvent lines; replace worn seals.
