Troubleshooting

Adapted from Shimadzu's Liquid Chromatography Mass Spectrometry Troubleshooting Guide. Before swapping parts:

  • Check the basics first: power, cable connections, mobile phase flowing with no air bubbles, LC pump pressure, ion source condition, roughing pump oil level, MS vacuum, gas cylinder/generator pressure.
  • Clearly define the problem (e.g. "loss of sensitivity", not just "it's not working") before you start changing things.
  • Change one variable at a time, and keep a record of what you tried.
  • During method development, note down normal retention times and operating pressures — that baseline makes it much easier to spot when something's drifted.
  • After a fix, inject a known sample and compare to previous data to confirm performance is actually restored.
