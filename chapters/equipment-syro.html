Equipment

Syro II Peptide Synthesiser

🟢 QUICK SUMMARY Fill solvent bottles and prime the lines, load the sequence, weigh out resin and amino acids (overshoot DIC/Oxyma by 10%), swell the resin, then run the synthesis. Check the lines aren't blocked before you start — that's the single most common thing that goes wrong.

Purpose

Automated solid-phase peptide synthesis at 0.02 mmol scale, in parallel — up to 96 peptides in a single run. This is the group's go-to instrument for multiplexed peptide synthesis.

When to Use

  • Multiple peptides in parallel (up to 96), at 0.02 mmol scale.

When Not to Use

  • A single peptide at larger scale — use the Alstra instead.
🔴 SAFETY Standard PPE and normal handling precautions for piperidine, DIC, and Oxyma apply — nothing beyond what's already assumed lab knowledge.

Preparation

  1. Turn Syro on at the switch.
  2. Log in to the computer (Keltic) and open the Syro application.
    🔵 IF THE APPLICATION WON'T OPEN Click Repair on the application and keep hitting Yes until it completes, then re-open the Syro application.
  3. Add your sequence list as a .txt file, longest sequence first, written N to C.
  4. In Syro: File > Library > Path of the .txt file (Sequence List).
  5. Remove any blank lines or trailing spaces at the bottom of the file, then press OK.
  6. Set the coupling method from the preset workfile dropdown — generally DIC-DIC_50C_30min.
  7. Check that the first cycle is the DIC-DIC_50C_30min_START sequence, and the remaining cycles use the correct coupling method.
  8. Tick fixed amino acid positions, unless you're adding an extra unnatural amino acid.
  9. Click Generate.
  10. In the Update Activities window that pops up, click Calc.
  11. Enter resin loading and equivalents (5), and tick "use concentration of 0.50 mol/L".
  12. Click Recalculate to get the resin amount in mg.
  13. Print the calculation table and start weighing out amino acids and solvents.

Required Materials

  • Resin (amount per the calculation table)
  • Amino acids (per sequence, dissolved and spun down in falcon tubes before loading)
  • DIC and Oxyma — weigh with a 10% overshoot so the needle can correctly detect the solvent level
  • DMF, WashDMF, 40% piperidine (line-priming and wash solvents)

Procedure

1. Prime the lines

  1. Open the manual window and select the FILL option.
  2. Move 10,000 µL of 40% piperidine and WashDMF to the right arm.
  3. Select the 40% piperidine/WashDMF reservoir, then the waste drum, to prime those lines.
  4. Move 10,000 µL of DMF to the left arm (outer blue circle).
  5. Select the DMF reservoir, then the waste drum, to prime that line.
    ❓ NEEDS CONFIRMATION The source document repeats "select the 40% piperidine/WashDMF reservoir" for this DMF-priming step too — that looks like a copy/paste slip rather than intentional. Please confirm this step should reference the DMF reservoir, not piperidine/WashDMF again.
  6. Click Update and Execute.
  7. Check the reactor vessels and lines aren't blocked.
  8. Add 1,000 µL of DMF to each reactor with the FILL option.
  9. Select Empty, then Update/Execute — confirm draining occurs.
  10. Lines are now primed and unblocked.

2. Weigh and swell resin

  1. Weigh resin out into reactor vials — see Appendix 1 for the plate-measure method.
  2. Swell the resin using the premade chemfile swell before start_KMD (Library > Chemfiles — path: Users\Syro\Documents\SyroXP\Library). This chemfile:
    • Fills the reaction vessels with 500 µL of DMF.
    • Reacts for 20 min at 75°C (includes 20 s vortexing / 2 min break cycles — both adjustable in the chemfile).
    • Empties for 0.5 min.
  3. Alternatively, run it manually: Manual > Fill > 500 µL WashDMF to the relevant reactors → Reaction (15 min, 75°C) → Empty (1 min).
    ❓ NEEDS CONFIRMATION Confirmed: the chemfile (20 min) and manual fallback (15 min) are intentionally different times, not a documentation error — kept as-is above. Flagging here only so a future editor doesn't "fix" this by mistake.
  4. This swelling step should be run before the peptide synthesis itself, input via the manual tab.

Cleaning

  • Empty all three waste drums after synthesis.
  • Wash amino acid / DIC / Oxyma tubes.

Shutdown

Switch off the machine once cleaning is complete.

Appendix 1 — Measuring Resin into Reactors

  1. Get the plate measures out of the Syro II accessory cupboard.
  2. Using the resin amount from the calculation table, find the combination of 20, 45, and 90 µL plates that yields the desired resin mass in a single reactor.
  3. Repeat for every reactor you're using; tape over the holes of any reactors you're not using.

Additional Notes

  • Adding an unnatural amino acid: Robot Settings > Use Settings > Edit under Name (right-click the amino acid position circle → Name).
  • Amino acid monoisotopic/average mass: remove all acid-labile protecting groups and H₂O (18.02) before entering the mass.
