Equipment

Syro II Peptide Synthesiser

● Published v0.2 Updated 2026-07-17
QUICK SUMMARY
Fill solvent bottles and prime the lines, load the sequence, weigh out resin and amino acids (overshoot DIC/Oxyma by 10%), swell the resin, then run the synthesis. Check the lines aren't blocked before you start — the single most common thing that goes wrong.

Purpose

Automated solid-phase peptide synthesis at 0.02 mmol scale, in parallel — up to 96 peptides in a single run. This is the group's go-to instrument for multiplexed peptide synthesis.

When to use

  • Multiple peptides in parallel (up to 96), at 0.02 mmol scale.

For a single peptide at larger scale, use the Alstra instead.

Safety

SAFETY
Standard PPE and normal handling precautions for piperidine, DIC and Oxyma apply — nothing beyond what's already assumed lab knowledge.

Preparation

  1. Turn Syro on at the switch.
  2. Log in to the computer (Keltic) and open the Syro application.
    IF THE APPLICATION WON'T OPEN
    Click Repair on the application and keep hitting Yes until it completes, then re-open.
  3. Add your sequence list as a .txt file, longest sequence first, written N to C.
  4. In Syro: File > Library > Path of the .txt file, then remove any blank lines / trailing spaces and press OK.
  5. Set the coupling method from the preset workfile dropdown — generally DIC-DIC_50C_30min.
  6. Check the first cycle is DIC-DIC_50C_30min_START and the rest use the correct method.
  7. Click Generate → in Update Activities click Calc.
  8. Enter resin loading and equivalents (5), tick "use concentration of 0.50 mol/L", then Recalculate for the resin amount in mg.
  9. Print the calculation table and start weighing out amino acids and solvents.

Required materials

  • Resin (amount per the calculation table).
  • Amino acids (per sequence, dissolved and spun down in falcon tubes before loading).
  • DIC and Oxyma — weigh with a 10% overshoot so the needle can correctly detect the solvent level.
  • DMF, WashDMF, 40% piperidine (line-priming and wash solvents).

Procedure

1. Prime the lines

  1. Open the manual window and select FILL.
  2. Move 10,000 µL of 40% piperidine and WashDMF to the right arm.
  3. Select the 40% piperidine/WashDMF reservoir, then the waste drum, to prime those lines.
  4. Move 10,000 µL of DMF to the left arm (outer blue circle) and prime that line via the DMF reservoir → waste drum.
  5. Click Update then Execute.
  6. Check the reactor vessels and lines aren't blocked.
  7. Add 1,000 µL of DMF to each reactor with FILL, then Empty → Update/Execute and confirm draining. Lines are now primed and unblocked.

2. Weigh and swell resin

  1. Weigh resin into reactor vials — see Appendix 1 for the plate-measure method.
  2. Swell using the premade chemfile swell before start_KMD (500 µL DMF, 20 min at 75°C, then a 0.5 min empty).
  3. Manual fallback: Fill 500 µL WashDMF → Reaction (15 min, 75°C) → Empty (1 min).
NOTE
The chemfile (20 min) and manual fallback (15 min) times are intentionally different, not a documentation error — left as-is on purpose.

Cleaning & shutdown

  • Empty all three waste drums after synthesis.
  • Wash amino acid / DIC / Oxyma tubes.
  • Switch off the machine once cleaning is complete.

Common mistakes

COMMON MISTAKES
  • Not checking/unblocking the lines before starting a run.
  • Getting the resin-to-plate-size calculation wrong (see Appendix 1).

Appendix 1 — Measuring resin into reactors

  1. Get the plate measures out of the Syro II accessory cupboard.
  2. Using the resin amount from the calculation table, find the combination of 20, 45 and 90 µL plates that yields the desired resin mass in a single reactor.
  3. Repeat for every reactor you're using; tape over the holes of any reactors you're not using.

Additional notes

  • Adding an unnatural amino acid: Robot Settings > Use Settings > Edit under Name (right-click the amino acid position circle → Name).
  • Amino acid monoisotopic/average mass: remove all acid-labile protecting groups and H₂O (18.02) before entering the mass.
